Startup Funding Stages and Investment Criteria
===============================================

PRE-SEED STAGE ($0-500K)
- Purpose: Idea validation, team building, market research
- Key Metrics: Problem-solution fit, team quality, market size
- Investment Criteria: Strong founding team, clear problem identification, large addressable market
- Typical Investors: Friends & family, angel investors, incubators
- Success Indicators: Customer interviews completed, MVP concept validated

SEED STAGE ($500K-2M)
- Purpose: MVP development, market testing, initial customer acquisition
- Key Metrics: Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR), Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Investment Criteria: Product-market fit, early customer traction, scalable business model
- Typical Investors: Seed funds, angel groups, early-stage VCs
- Success Indicators: $10K+ MRR, 20%+ month-over-month growth

SERIES A ($2M-15M)
- Purpose: Product-market fit validation, team scaling, market expansion
- Key Metrics: ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue), burn rate, runway, customer retention
- Investment Criteria: Proven product-market fit, strong growth metrics, experienced team
- Typical Investors: Venture capital firms, growth funds
- Success Indicators: $1M+ ARR, 100%+ year-over-year growth

SERIES B ($15M-100M)
- Purpose: Market expansion, operational scaling, international growth
- Key Metrics: Unit economics, market share, competitive positioning
- Investment Criteria: Strong unit economics, market leadership, proven scalability
- Typical Investors: Growth equity firms, late-stage VCs
- Success Indicators: Positive unit economics, market leadership position

SERIES C+ ($100M+)
- Purpose: International expansion, acquisitions, IPO preparation
- Key Metrics: EBITDA, market dominance, strategic partnerships
- Investment Criteria: Market leadership, strong financials, clear exit path
- Typical Investors: Private equity, sovereign wealth funds, strategic investors
- Success Indicators: Market dominance, strong profitability, clear exit strategy

KEY INVESTMENT METRICS
======================

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
- Definition: Annualized value of recurring revenue contracts
- Target: $1M+ for Series A, $10M+ for Series B
- Growth Rate: 100%+ year-over-year for early stages

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- Definition: Total cost to acquire one customer
- Target: CAC < LTV/3 for sustainable growth
- Optimization: Focus on organic growth channels

LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)
- Definition: Total revenue from customer over relationship
- Target: LTV > 3x CAC for healthy unit economics
- Calculation: Average revenue per customer Ã— customer lifespan

Burn Rate
- Definition: Monthly cash consumption
- Target: 12-18 months runway minimum
- Management: Balance growth with sustainability

Runway
- Definition: Time until cash runs out
- Target: 18+ months for early stages
- Planning: Raise next round 6-12 months before running out

INVESTMENT EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
===============================

TEAM ASSESSMENT (30% weight)
- Founder experience and track record
- Team completeness and expertise
- Execution capability and adaptability
- Network and industry connections

MARKET OPPORTUNITY (25% weight)
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) size
- Market growth rate and trends
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Regulatory environment assessment

PRODUCT/SOLUTION (20% weight)
- Problem-solution fit validation
- Technology differentiation and IP
- Scalability and technical feasibility
- User experience and adoption potential

BUSINESS MODEL (15% weight)
- Revenue model clarity and sustainability
- Unit economics and profitability potential
- Customer acquisition strategy
- Pricing power and market positioning

TRACTION & METRICS (10% weight)
- Customer growth and retention
- Revenue growth and predictability
- Market validation and feedback
- Operational efficiency metrics

RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
=========================

TECHNICAL RISKS
- Technology maturity and reliability
- Scalability challenges and limitations
- Security and compliance requirements
- Technical debt and maintenance costs

MARKET RISKS
- Market timing and adoption barriers
- Competitive pressure and differentiation
- Regulatory changes and compliance
- Economic cycles and market volatility

EXECUTION RISKS
- Team scaling and management challenges
- Operational complexity and costs
- Customer acquisition and retention
- International expansion challenges

FINANCIAL RISKS
- Cash flow management and burn rate
- Funding availability and terms
- Unit economics and profitability
- Exit strategy and valuation multiples

INVESTMENT RECOMMENDATION FRAMEWORK
===================================

STRONG BUY (Score: 8-10)
- Exceptional team with proven track record
- Large, growing market with clear competitive advantage
- Strong product-market fit with significant traction
- Clear path to profitability and exit
- Recommendation: Invest maximum allocation

BUY (Score: 6-7.9)
- Strong team with relevant experience
- Good market opportunity with moderate competition
- Validated product-market fit with growing traction
- Path to profitability within 18-24 months
- Recommendation: Invest standard allocation

HOLD (Score: 4-5.9)
- Adequate team with some concerns
- Moderate market opportunity with high competition
- Unclear product-market fit or limited traction
- Uncertain path to profitability
- Recommendation: Monitor closely, consider follow-on

SELL (Score: 1-3.9)
- Weak team or execution concerns
- Small or declining market opportunity
- Poor product-market fit or no traction
- No clear path to profitability
- Recommendation: Avoid investment, consider exit

EXIT STRATEGY CONSIDERATIONS
============================

ACQUISITION EXIT
- Strategic value to larger companies
- Market consolidation opportunities
- Technology or team acquisition potential
- Typical timeline: 3-7 years
- Expected returns: 3-10x investment

IPO EXIT
- Market conditions and investor appetite
- Company size and financial metrics
- Regulatory compliance and reporting
- Typical timeline: 5-10 years
- Expected returns: 5-20x investment

SECONDARY SALE
- Liquidity for early investors
- Company maturity and market position
- Secondary market dynamics
- Typical timeline: 4-8 years
- Expected returns: 2-8x investment
